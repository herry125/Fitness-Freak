COURSES THAT ARE SUITABLE FOR DISEASES




Scoliosis:

Scoliosis is a medical condition that causes the spine to curve sideways. While aerobic exercise is not a direct treatment for scoliosis, it can help improve overall health and well-being. Its important to note that before starting any new exercise program, its best to consult with a doctor or physical therapist who can recommend personalized exercises based on your specific needs.



Anxiety:
By lowering general stress levels, increasing and stabilizing mood, enhancing sleep, and boosting self-esteem, aerobic exercise can help control anxiety. Here are some effective aerobic workouts for anxiety management: Swimming , Biking , Running , rapidwalking , Tennis , Dancing These exercises can be done three to five days a week for at least 30 minutes each time. Exercise helps to reduce muscle tension, distract you from the source of your anxiety, and boost your body's production of key anti-anxiety neurochemicals like serotonin, gamma aminobutyric acid , brain-derived neurotrophic factor , and endocannabinoids.

Cervical Radioculopathy:
Pinched Nerve Exercises: Most people with cervical radiculopathy work with an orthopedic physical therapist or one who is a musculoskeletal specialist. Your physical therapist may use various treatments to manage your cervical radiculopathy. Exercise and postural correction should be your main tools in the treatment of your cervical radiculopathy. The goals of rehab for a pinched nerve are to restore normal neck motion, improve strength and posture, and give you a self-care strategy to keep pressure off the nerve. Before starting any exercise program for cervical radiculopathy, check in with your healthcare provider to ensure that it is safe for you to proceed. If you have pain in your arm, monitor your symptoms as you exercise. If symptoms of pain and numbness or tingling get worse in your arm as you exercise, stop. This is a sign that the nerve is getting compressed more.


Depression:
The benefits of aerobic exercise for depression are numerous. The main advantages include improved life satisfaction, stronger self-esteem, and fewer negative thoughts. It helps control depressive symptoms.It raises scholastic success, cognitive functioning, and memory. Its impact on depressive symptoms is moderate, even on par with psychotherapy. It causes the release of feel-good endorphins and other organic brain chemicals that can improve your mood. It boosts blood flow to the brain, improving sound cognitive performance and stable brain chemistry. 150 minutes a week of exercise is advised by researchers to help mental health conditions like anxiety and sadness. Biking, dancing, gardening, golf, housework, running, low-impact aerobics, and playing tennis are a few examples of moderate exercise.


Frozen Shoulder:
When a person has frozen shoulder, the shoulder joint stiffens and hurts, making it difficult to move the arm. Injury, surgery, or other medical issues may be the root of it. Exercises that increase heart rate are not advised for those with frozen shoulder since they may endanger the joint even more. Instead, it is advised to perform strengthening and stretching activities to increase range of motion and lessen pain.
