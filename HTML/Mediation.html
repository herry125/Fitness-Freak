MEDIATION

Mediation is a method of alternative dispute resolution (ADR). It involves working with a trained mediator who helps to facilitate communication between parties involved in a legal dispute. The purpose of mediation is to help the parties find a compromise so they can avoid going to court and asking a judge or a jury to decide their issues. Mediation is also defined as a structured, interactive process where an impartial third party neutrally assists disputing parties in resolving conflict through the use of specialized communication and negotiation techniques2. All participants in mediation are encouraged to actively participate in the process.

BENEFITS OF MEDIATION

Mediation is cost-Efficient.

Mediation is typically more cost-effective than pursuing a legal battle in court. It can save both time and money for all parties involved.

Sustainability.

Mediated agreements are often more sustainable because they are tailored to the parties' needs and are built on mutual understanding and collaboration

Empowerment

Mediation empowers parties to have control over the resolution of their dispute. They actively participate in the process and the decision-making, which can be more satisfying and enduring than having a resolution imposed by a judge.

Mediation is confidential.

Mediation is a private process, whereas legal disputes often become part of the public record when brought to court. This can be especially important in cases involving sensitive or confidential information.

Conflict Resolution

Mediation is a voluntary and non-adversarial process that allows parties in a dispute to work together to find mutually agreeable solutions. This often leads to better relationships and a sense of closure.

Flexibility

Mediation allows for more flexible solutions. Parties can create agreements that may not be possible through litigation, as they can address unique concerns and needs.

Preservation of Relationships

In personal and business disputes, mediation often helps preserve relationships that might otherwise be damaged by adversarial legal proceedings.

Reduced Stress

Mediation is typically less confrontational and emotionally taxing than litigation, which can be emotionally and mentally draining.

Availability of Solutions.

Mediation can often lead to more amicable and practical solutions that meet the needs of all parties, while litigation may not offer such flexibility.

Satisfaction

Parties tend to be more satisfied with mediated settlements because they have actively participated in the resolution process and had a say in the outcome.



IMPORTANCE OF MEDIATION

Mediation is important because it provides an alternative to litigation and can be used in a variety of disputes, including family law, employment, and business disputes. A process in which the parties agree to an impartial facilitator (a neutral third party) to assist them to reach a voluntary settlement of the issues in dispute. The mediator does not make a decision, and the parties may terminate the process at any time. The goal of this effort is to resolve conflicts through the use of specialized negotiation and communication techniques In summary, mediation can be an effective way to resolve disputes outside of court. It can be less expensive and quicker than traditional litigation, and it allows the parties involved to have more control over the outcome of their dispute. However, it may not be effective in all situations.
