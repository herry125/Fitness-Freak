1.SCOLIOSIS:

Scoliosis is a medical condition that causes the spine to curve sideways. While aerobic exercise is not a direct treatment for scoliosis, it can help improve overall health and well-being. According to WebMD, some exercises that can help build strength and reduce pain in people with scoliosis include:

Exercises For Scoliosis:

  • Pelvic Tilts:
    This exercise helps strengthen the muscles in your lower back.
  • Sitting Rotation Stretch:
    This stretch can help improve flexibility, which can reduce some of the back pain you may be experiencing.
  • Cat/Cow:
    This stretch promotes flexibility throughout your body.
  • Bird Dog:
    This exercise strengthens your core muscles and improves your posture.
  • Plank:
    This exercise strengthens your core muscles, which can help stabilize the spine.
  • Kettlebell Suitcase Deadlift:
    This exercise helps strengthen your core muscles and improve your overall posture
It's important to note that before starting any new exercise program, it's best to consult with a doctor or physical therapist who can recommend personalized exercises based on your specific needs

BASIC DO'S AND DON'Ts FOR SCOLIOSIS
DO's:
  • Stay active while approaching certain activities with caution.
  • Yoga: Yoga can help stretch out tense muscles in your neck, lower back, and legs.
  • Use a heating pad to alleviate scoliosis pain.
  • Sleep, get up, and use computers correctly.
DON'Ts:
  • Sports/exercises that negatively impact the spine: Avoid sports/exercises that negatively impact the spine, interfere with treatment, or risk spinal injury.
  • Weightlifting: Lifting heavy weights can put a lot of strain on your spine, which can exacerbate your scoliosis.
  • Long-distance running: Running long distances can cause your spine to twist and turn, which can worsen your scoliosis.
  • Long jump: Jumping long distances can put a lot of pressure on your spine, which can be harmful if you have scoliosis.
  • Horseback riding: Horseback riding involves a lot of twisting and turning, which can be harmful if you have scoliosis.
  • Deep backbends: Deep backbends can put a lot of pressure on your spine, which can exacerbate your scoliosis.
  • Gymnastics: Gymnastics involves a lot of twisting and turning, which can be harmful if you have scoliosis.
  • High jumps: High jumps can put a lot of pressure on your spine, which can be harmful if you have scoliosis.
  • Diving: Diving involves a lot of twisting and turning, which can be harmful if you have scoliosis.
  • Certain dance maneuvers: Certain dance maneuvers involve a lot of twisting and turning, which can be harmful if you have scoliosis.

2.STROKE PATIENTS:


Exercises For stroke patients:

    This exercise is great for strengthening the muscles of your hip, leg, and core. It also facilitates proper weight shifting and control overbalance. Repeat this exercise 8-10 times on each side click here for video

BASIC DO'S AND DON'Ts FOR stroke patients
DO's:
  • 1.Do talk to your doctor before starting any exercise program.
  • 2. Do start with simple exercises and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workout.
  • 3. Do focus on exercises that improve your balance, coordination, and flexibility.
DON'Ts:
  • 1.Don't skip warm-up exercises. Cold muscles are more prone to injury than warm muscles. Before you start exercising, warm up with five to ten minutes of brisk walking or other aerobic activity .
  • 2.Don't rush. Move the weight in an unhurried, controlled fashion .
  • 3. Don't pull or strain any weakened limbs .
  • 4. Don't continue exercising if you feel pain or discomfort.

3.FROZEN SHOULDER:

When a person has frozen shoulder, the shoulder joint stiffens and hurts, making it difficult to move the arm. Injury, surgery, or other medical issues may be the root of it. Exercises that increase heart rate are not advised for those with frozen shoulder since they may endanger the joint even more. Instead, it is advised to perform strengthening and stretching activities to increase range of motion and lessen pain.

Exercises for frozen shoulder

  • Pendulum stretch:
    While standing, stoop slightly and hang the affected arm down. Swing the arm in a foot-diameter-or-smaller circle.
  • towel stretch:
    Stretching with a towel involves holding one end behind your back while grabbing the other end with the other hand
  • Doorway stretch:
    Stand in an open doorway with your hands at shoulder height on the door frame. Keep your feet still while you lean forward and flex your chest muscles.
  • Cross-body reach:
    Raise your injured arm at the elbow and bring it across your body while gently applying pressure to the shoulder with your good arm.
  • Armpit stretch:
    Place the affected arm, with the hand pointing down, on a shelf or table. When you feel a stretch in your armpit, slowly bend your knees and lower your torso.
  • External rotation:
    Bend your elbows at a 90-degree angle while holding a rubber workout band between your hands. When rotating the injured arm, keep your elbow close to your side.
Frozen shoulder is a condition that affects the mobility of the shoulder joint and can be quite painful. The following are some do's and don'ts for exercises that can help manage frozen shoulder.
BASIC DO'S AND DON'Ts FOR FROZEN SHOULDER
DO's:
  • 1.Take pain relievers as directed by your doctor or health care provider.
  • 2.Follow a physical therapy program and exercise regularly, but gently.
  • 3.Use an ice pack for pain relief, several times a day.
  • 4.Have a positive mindset, gratitude, and patience.

Your physical therapist may use various treatments to manage your cervical radiculopathy. Exercise and postural correction should be your main tools in the treatment of your cervical radiculopathy. The goals of rehab for a pinched nerve are to restore normal neck motion, improve strength and posture, and give you a self-care strategy to keep pressure off the nerve. Before starting any exercise program for cervical radiculopathy, check in with your healthcare provider to ensure that it is safe for you to proceed. If you have pain in your arm, monitor your symptoms as you exercise. If symptoms of pain and numbness or tingling get worse in your arm as you exercise, stop. This is a sign that the nerve is getting compressed more. If arm pain decreases and shoulder blade or neck pain increase as you exercise, keep going. This is a phenomenon called centralization, and the centralization of pain that occurs as you exercise is a good sign. When exercising, keep an eye on your symptoms if you have arm ache. 5.ANXIETY:

By lowering general stress levels, increasing and stabilizing mood, enhancing sleep, and boosting self-esteem, aerobic exercise can help control anxiety. Here are some effective aerobic workouts for anxiety management:


Exercises For Anxiety:

  • Swimming
  • Running.
  • rapid walking
  • Dancing
  • Tennis
  • Biking
These exercises can be done three to five days a week for at least 30 minutes each time. Exercise helps to reduce muscle tension, distract you from the source of your anxiety, and boost your body's production of key anti-anxiety neurochemicals like serotonin, gamma aminobutyric acid (GABA), brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), and endocannabinoids.

6.DEPRESSION:

The benefits of aerobic exercise for depression are numerous. The main advantages include improved life satisfaction, stronger self-esteem, and fewer negative thoughts. Its impact on depressive symptoms is moderate, even on par with psychotherapy. It causes the release of feel-good endorphins and other organic brain chemicals that can improve your mood.

Exercises For Depression:

  • 150 minutes a week of exercise is advised by researchers to help mental health conditions like anxiety and sadness.
  • Biking.
  • Gardening
  • Dancing
  • Running
  • Tennis
  • Low-impact aerobics
It's important to note that before starting any new exercise program, it's best to consult with a doctor or physical therapist who can recommend personalized exercises based on your specific needs

BASIC DO'S AND DON'Ts FOR ANXIETY
DO's:
  • 1.Start with low-intensity exercises such as walking, cycling, or swimming.
  • 2. Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workout.
  • 3. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise, five days a week.
  • 4..Exercise with a friend or family member to stay motivated.
