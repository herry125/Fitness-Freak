DIFFERENCE BETWEEN CARDIO AND AEROBIC EXERCISES:
Although the terms aerobic and cardiac exercise are sometimes used synonymously, they refer to significantly different mechanisms. Both forms of exercise raise heart rate, blood supply to muscles, and oxygen levels. Cardio refers to heart-related activities, whereas aerobic exercise refers to workouts that utilize oxygen . In conclusion, aerobic and cardio exercise refer to somewhat different mechanisms, yet they both ultimately refer to the same thing. Both forms of exercise raise heart rate, blood supply to muscles, and oxygen levels.

Aerobic Exercises



What Are Aerobic Activities?

Cardiovascular exercise, often known as aerobic exercise, works your heart out. Your blood circulates swiftly throughout your body during aerobic exercise, and your lungs absorb more oxygen as a result. Since the word "aerobic" means "with oxygen," the amount of oxygen that reaches your muscles depends on how much you breathe. Your muscles move because of the oxygen in them, which gives you the power to exercise. Utilizing oxygen and your body's stored carbohydrates, lipids, and proteins, your body produces this energy. Aerobic activities can be done for extended periods of time depending on their intensity.

BENEFITS OF AEROBIC/CARDIO


Enhances cardiovascular health:

Exercise makes your heart stronger and increases the effectiveness of its blood-pump function. By increasing "good" high-density lipoprotein (HDL) cholesterol and lowering "bad" low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol levels in the blood, cardiovascular exercise can also help lower blood pressure and keep your arteries free.

Blood Pressure Reduction:

You may be able to manage your high blood pressure symptoms with cardiovascular exercise, People can reduce their blood pressure in several ways, including diet, exercise, and blood pressure medication. These changes may start to reduce blood pressure within a few weeks.

Improves Sleep:

Regular exercise and instruction on proper sleep hygiene are excellent treatments for insomnia. After 16 weeks of aerobic exercise, participants answered questions on their overall mood and sleep before completing the questionnaires .

Controls Weight:

It's possible that you've heard that food and exercise are the foundations of weight loss. However, doing only aerobic activity can be enough to help you lose weight and keep it off. In one studyTrusted Source, researchers urged overweight volunteers to continue eating the same amount of food but to exercise for five times a week for ten months, burning between 400 and 600 calories.

HARMS OF AEROBIC/CARDIO


Muscle soreness:

Starting a new fitness regimen or upping the intensity of your workouts frequently results in muscle soreness. Within a few days, it typically fades away without treatment

Heart attack:

Intense exercise can sometimes cause an increase in blood pressure which can lead to a heart attack. If you already suffer from high blood pressure, this risk is even greater. High blood pressure can damage arteries and other parts of the cardiovascular system, leading to an increased risk of heart attack and stroke

Heat exhuastion:

Heat exhaustion is a condition that develops when your body overheats as a result of extended exposure to hot conditions or vigorous exercise. Dizziness, nausea, rapid heartbeat, and profuse sweating are among the symptoms

Overuse injuries:

People who engage in high-impact activities like jogging or leaping frequently suffer from overuse injuries. When you exert too much force on your body without allowing it enough time to heal, injuries like these develop
